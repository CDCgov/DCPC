Use Case

There are four pathology services Stanford, OpenNLP, Gate and cTAKES.

  • The services address the needs of cancer registries and other epidemiology organisations to convert text into categorical data or codes so they can more effectively identify what is the same although expressed in different language. Using the Pathology Coding Service will enable organisations to more readily complete this conversion from text to codes.
  • The four services can be used to demonstrate differences between different pathology datasets using different pipelines.

Identified Semantics

The pathology services are designed to supply codes for 5 pieces of information:

  • Body site of the cancer
  • Histology
  • Grade of disease
  • Behavior of the cancer
  • Laterality or side of the body where it is situated

The Clinical Entity Recognition (CER) service supplies 22 different entities of information including structural information about the document, which is necessary for the finding the best location to extract the desired content.

The different clinical entities identified in the CER service need to be culled down to those that are most useful for deriving the wanted codes.

Approach to Address the Use Case

This use case applies to organisations that want to extract content from pathology reports and code that content. The service is split into two services so that the Clinical Entity Recognition can be assessed independently from the coding process.
