About CLEW

The Project

The Clinical Language Engineering Workbench (CLEW) project is a joint effort between Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and Food and Drug Administration (FDA) funded by Assistant Secretary for Planning and Evaluation (ASPE).

The purpose of the project is to research the feasibility of Natural Language Processing ( NLP) technology, its status and potential use within the clinical domain.

The Workbench

The CLEW workbench incorporates findings and applied research from the CLEW project. It provides a way for public health and PCOR researchers to:

  • Use, develop, experiment with and refine NLP models.
  • Install your engineered clinical NLP pipeline on the workbench instance for use as a public service, or install it in your local working environment.
  • Use one of the publicly available clinical NLP pipelines services for your application or modify one of these services for your particular use case using the workbench.
  • Develop a clinical NLP pipeline from scratch from available open source NLP tools.

The Website
