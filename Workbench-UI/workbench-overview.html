Services

Services are provided for clinical NLP and machine-learning pipelines developed to analyze unstructured cancer pathology reports and safety surveillance data.

These services were developed by NLP experts to address your specific needs. The services can be run in real-time mode or called from an external application to process a block of clinical text from the designated clinical documents. As new services are developed, they will be added to this list of available shared services for use. More >

CLEW Instance of the LAPPS Grid

The CLEW/LAPPS environment provides open source NLP and machine learning tools for developing new NLP pipelines. It includes all the tools on LAPPS plus additional clinical processing tools to make adjustments to LAPPS pipeline tools for clinical processing. More >

Investigations of LAPPS Tools

Read about some of the workflow investigations conducted in the CLEW project

Behaviour of GATE Chunker and Metamap Concept Mapping in LAPPS
Summarizes results from testing the GATE Chunker. It also summarizes the performance of NLM’s Metathesaurus (aka Metamap) using the chunker results.

Behaviour of POS Tagging in LAPPS - Results Report
Investigates the difference between different existing POS Tagger tools in LAPPS of Stanford POS Tagger and OpenNLP POS Tagger. The assessment is performed on the Final Diagnosis section of pathology reports.

Behaviour of Tokenizers in LAPPS
Assesses three different tokenizers in LAPPS, Stanford Tokenizer, OpenNLP Tokenizer and GATE Tokenizer. Limitations for each tokenizer and the ways to correct their weaknesses are described in this report.

Operating NLP Pipelines on the CLEW
Investigates three different workflows in LAPPS: Stanford, OpenNLP and GATE with a comparison of the performance of these three workflows.
