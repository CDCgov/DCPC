Overview of NLP Basics

Get a general understanding of Natural Language Process (NLP), it's history and terminology along with the basic architecture of the CLEW platform.

When you finish the NLP basics we recommend that you walk through the demos. In addition, you may be interested in viewing some of the "Workbench Support" information that is geared towards the advanced user. This advanced support information is in the "Use the Workbench" section.

Table of Contents

  • 1. What is NLP

    Natural Language Processing (NLP) is the field of computational methods for processing the linguistic characteristics of textual documents. In this section, start to learn basic NLP terminology, common NLP approaches, the importance of context in processing linguistic characteristics and a brief history of NLP.

  • 2. The NLP Pipeline

    NLP relies on a linear processing stream of modules each serving a particular function. This sequence forms a pipeline where the output of one module becomes the input to another. In this section, learn about these modules and why a linear pipeline approach is used in NLP.

  • 3. Building a NLP Solution

    The NLP pipeline is built to meet the requirements for your domain. The early modules in the pipeline tend to be conventional with a tokenizer and sentence boundary detector. Further into the pipeline many other choices are available, such as, an unknown word validator, spelling corrector, Part-of-Speech tagger, grammar analyser, phrase chunker, etc. In this section, learn about some of the different approaches to building a solution.

  • 4. CLEW & NLP

    CLEW is a platform to support clinical NLP. In this section, learn about what the CLEW platform provides along with key concepts associated with CLEW.
